Here's the full technical plan. A few highlights worth calling out:

**Gauge** — Rendered as a 2D canvas arc with a red→yellow→green→yellow→red gradient sweep. The pointer is a DOM div rotated via GSAP's `sine.inOut` yoyo tween (not canvas-drawn), making it trivially easy to kill and freeze on click. Depth maps linearly from edge=minDepth to center=maxDepth.

**3D without assets** — Everything is primitive Three.js geometry: half-torus for the hook, stacked boxes/spheres for the fisherman, shape-coded fish per rarity. The fishing line is a dynamic `THREE.Line` redrawn every frame between rod tip and hook. Legendary fish get a child `PointLight` for a natural underwater glow.

**Audio** — 12 named audio slots listed with exact trigger points. Howler.js handles looping (`hook_descend`, `hook_ascend`, `bg_music`) with clean stop-on-exit-state calls.

**VFX tiering** — The Rare/Epic/Legendary escalation is deliberate: Legendary triggers screen shake, edge flash, 32-particle burst, and a huge glowing badge — common fish get nothing fancy. This makes depth feel genuinely rewarding.

**End screen** — Strictly only a CTA button opening `window.open('', '_blank')`. Round counter stays hidden throughout.
